Food Storage Tips

Learn how to store your food properly to extend its shelf life and reduce waste.

Fruits and Vegetables

Store fruits and vegetables separately. Some fruits emit ethylene gas, which can cause vegetables to spoil faster.

  • Keep apples, bananas, and tomatoes away from other produce as they emit more ethylene gas.
  • Store leafy greens with a paper towel to absorb excess moisture.
  • Keep root vegetables in a cool, dark place rather than the refrigerator.
Meat and Dairy

Keep meat and dairy products in the coldest part of your refrigerator to maintain freshness.

  • Store raw meat on the bottom shelf to prevent drips onto other foods.
  • Keep cheese wrapped in breathable paper rather than plastic wrap.
  • Store milk and dairy away from the door where temperature fluctuates more.

Meal Planning

Plan your meals for the week to avoid buying unnecessary items and wasting food.

Creating a Meal Plan

List out meals for each day and create a shopping list based on the ingredients needed.

  • Check your inventory before shopping to avoid buying duplicates.
  • Plan meals that can use similar ingredients to reduce waste.
  • Consider batch cooking and freezing portions for future use.
Smart Shopping

Buy only what you need and will use within a reasonable timeframe.

  • Shop with a list and stick to it to avoid impulse purchases.
  • Be realistic about how much fresh produce you'll consume.
  • Check expiration dates before purchasing.

Composting

Learn how to compost your food scraps to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil.

What to Compost

Fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and eggshells are great for composting.

  • Green materials: vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, grass clippings
  • Brown materials: dry leaves, newspaper, cardboard
  • Avoid: meat, dairy, oils, and processed foods
Starting a Compost Bin

Begin with a suitable container and alternate layers of green and brown materials.

  • Choose between outdoor composting or indoor vermicomposting.
  • Maintain the right moisture level - damp like a wrung-out sponge.
  • Turn your compost regularly to aerate it and speed decomposition.

Environmental Impact

Understand how food waste affects our planet and why reducing it matters.

Carbon Footprint

Food waste is responsible for about 8% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

  • Food decomposing in landfills produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas.
  • Resources used in food production (water, energy, land) are wasted when food is discarded.
  • Transportation and processing of wasted food adds to carbon emissions.
